American Academy of Music

Center City - Philadelphia PA
The American Academy of Music is the oldest opera house in the United States still used for its original purpose, with a horseshoe‑shaped auditorium and acoustics designed to rival La Scala in Milan. Having hosted names like Tchaikovsky, Stravinsky, Rachmaninoff, Mahler, Pavarotti, Piaf, and Pavlova make this concert hall a living museum of musical history, and likely the finest surviving example of mid‑19th‑century American theater architecture.
