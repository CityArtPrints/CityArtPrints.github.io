Massachusetts Horticultural Society

*CUSTOM ARTWORK*  |  Fenway-Kenmore |  Boston MA
Horticultural Hall opened in 1901 as the purpose-built exhibition, lecture, and office space. Built in the English Renaissance Revival style, this incredible structure was built for the esteemed organization who's members developed fruits which later become commonplace in the American diet, such as the Concord grape and Bartlett pear. The Society also promoted scientific knowledge by disseminating scientific papers, including the then-controversial work of Charles Darwin. Now under the care of Northeastern University, today the building serves as a home for a variety of local businesses and organizations in another successful adaptation of a treasured Boston Landmark.
