Symphony Hall

Fenway-Kenmore | Boston MA
Modeled after the Gewandhaus in Leipzig, this stunning symphony hall is one of the first auditoria designed in accordance with scientifically derived acoustical principles, hiring a young physics professor from Harvard as a consultant. The elongated narrow shape, sloped walls, and meticulously chosen building materials, combined with careful placement of decorative elements put Boston's Symphony Hall among the finest venues for classical acoustics anywhere in the world.
